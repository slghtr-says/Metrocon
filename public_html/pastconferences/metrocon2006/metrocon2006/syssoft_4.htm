C++ in Safety-Critical Applications - the JSF Coding Standard
Dr. Bjarne Stroustrup and Dr. Kevin P. Carroll
Texas A&M University, Lockheed Martin Aeronautics Company

JSF++ is a coding standard for use of C++ in safety-critical applications used by Lockheed-Martin Areo and others for software for the Joint Strike Fighter.  The philosophy of JSF++ is basically an extension of C++'s philosophy to C.  That is, by providing safer alternatives to unsafe facilities, you can avoid known problems with low level features. That is, programs should be written in a (safer) subset of (a better behaved and better-specified) superset of C++.

The JSF++ is specifically designed to address undefined behavior and programming problems by restricting programmers to a better specified, more analyzable, and easier to read (and write) subset of C++. The idea is to eliminate large groups of problems by eliminating their root causes. For example, passing arrays between functions as pointers is simply banned, eliminating dozens of known problems. While allowing a large subset of ISO C++ features, the JSF++ is far safer than any simple C or C++ subset (e.g. MISRA C) could be.

This talk presents the principles of JSF++ and shows examples of the use and implications of individual rules.

Bjarne Stroustrup is the designer and original implementer of C++ and the author of "The C++ Programming Language" ( 1st edition 1985, 2nd edition 1991, 3rd edition, 1997, "special" edition 2000) and The Design and Evolution of C++. His research interests include distributed systems, design, programming techniques, software development tools, and programming languages.

About the Speakers:

Kevin Carroll is a Senior Staff Engineer at the Lockheed Martin Aeronautics Company. He received M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in computer science from Texas A&M University. Currently he is a member of the Joint Strike Fighter (JSF) Mission Systems Software Management Team where his responsibilities range from the design and deployment of reusable software components to the development of coding standards. In particular, he played a key role in guiding the Lockheed Martin effort to use C++ in safety-critical avionics systems on the internationally-oriented JSF program.
