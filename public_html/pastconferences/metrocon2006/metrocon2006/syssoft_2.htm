Development of High-Assurance Distributed Real-Time Embedded Systems
Farokh B. Bastani, Ph.D.
University of Texas at Dallas

In spite of over three decades of research, embedded software development is still a manual-intensive and error-prone process.  At the same time, the rapidly increasing number of critical embedded systems and their growing complexity pose an urgent need for high-assurance, high-productivity software engineering techniques.  Applications of distributed real-time embedded systems range from emergency response systems to defense systems, biomedical systems, transportation systems, etc.  These systems must be highly reliable since they are often used in harsh environments that are susceptible to hardware failures and even deliberate physical attacks.  They must also meet stringent performance constraints, including real-time processing and communication, and have the ability to operate effectively under severe power, weight, and memory constraints.

All these complexities lead to great difficulties in manually developing the software for these critical systems.  One attractive approach is to exploit automated code synthesis and dependability assurance methods.  However, existing synthesis and assurance methods require a steep learning curve and are error-prone.  To cope with these challenges, we are developing a three-pronged strategy namely, (1) aspect-oriented decomposition, (2) automated code synthesis and testing, and (3) dynamic health monitoring and adaptation.  An automated planning process is used to select, instantiate, and compose existing code fragments together to synthesize the code for each aspect.

To enhance the survivability of the system, its dependability requirements are decomposed into an orthogonal set of microservices that can be automatically customized and composed with the rest of the system.  The composition framework incorporates distributed logic to achieve highly survivable communication and coordination mechanisms for dealing with transient as well as permanent failures and preventing communication or processing hotspots.  The framework incorporates dynamic system health-monitoring and adaptation techniques for continuously assessing the health of the system and adapting it in real-time to cope with adverse conditions.

Based on the decomposition and synthesis process, a combination of formal, statistical, and AI reasoning techniques is used to achieve high-confidence certification of the dependability of the overall system.  These can be integrated into a programming environment for supporting the automated decomposition, synthesis, and composition of these types of specifications.  A prototype system is being developed to evaluate the approach for distributed real-time process-control systems.

Dr. Farokh B. Bastani received the B.Tech. degree in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay, and the M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science from the University of California, Berkeley.

He is currently a Professor of Computer Science at the University of Texas at Dallas.  Dr. Bastani's research interests include various aspects of ultrahigh dependable systems, especially automated software synthesis and testing, embedded real-time process-control and telecommunications systems, and high-assurance systems engineering.  He is currently an emeritus EIC of IEEE-TKDE and is on the editorial board of the International Journal of Artificial Intelligence Tools, the International Journal of Knowledge and Information Systems, and the Springer-Verlag series on Knowledge and Information Management.  He was the program co-chair of the 1997 IEEE Symposium on Reliable Distributed Systems, 1998 IEEE International Symposium on Software Reliability Engineering, 1999 IEEE Knowledge and Data Engineering Workshop, 1999 International Symposium on Autonomous Decentralized Systems, and the program chair of the 1995 IEEE International Conference on Tools with Artificial Intelligence.  He has been on the program and steering committees of several conferences and workshops and on the editorial boards of the IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering, IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering, and the Oxford University Press High Integrity Systems Journal.
