Advancing New Technologies at the Human Ocular Surface: The Search for Biomarkers
Michelle Senchyna, Ph.D.
Senior Scientist III
Alcon Research, Ltd.

With the rapidly advancing ability to simultaneously measure multiple biomarkers at the genetic and / or protein level, a major thrust of pharmaceutical research is the adoption and optimization of such technologies to advance the understanding of diseases processes and identification of new drug targets. Complexities in sample acquisition, sample type, nanotechnology and clinical application are all highlighted in the quest for biomarker identification and as such have necessitated a multi-disciplinary approach spanning many fields of expertise. All these concepts can be encapsulated in this discussion, which will focus on the search for biomarkers involved in human external ocular diseases such as dry eye, inflammation and allergy. Key topics of interest will include the complexity of the human tear film, the collection and use of extremely small amounts of biological samples and the logistics of using biomarker research in a human clinical setting. On a more global scale, various approaches, pitfalls and value of multiplexed protein and / or genetic analysis will be reviewed and discussed in the context of expanding our ability to think in biological pathways or networks. Lastly, validation of both methods and clinical application will be reviewed.

Dr. Michelle Senchyna received her Ph.D. in Medical Science from McMaster University (Hamilton, ON, CAD) in 1998, after which she joined the University of Waterloo School of Optometry as an Assistant Professor. Her research focused on two facets of the human ocular surface: 1) compatibility of novel biomaterials for use as contact lenses and 2) ocular surface disease (dry eye). Dr. Senchyna worked closely with leading industry partners as well as a muti-disciplinary host of collaborators merging molecular biology, immunology, material engineering and physics.  After being granted Tenure and Promotion in 2003, Dr. Senchyna joined Alcon Research Ltd (Ft Worth, TX), where she again is working on a multi-disciplinary team integrating clinical and biological sciences to develop novel strategies for the diagnosis and assessment of treatment efficacy of dry eye.
