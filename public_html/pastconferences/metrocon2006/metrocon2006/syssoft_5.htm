Emerging Telecommunication Networks and Services
Anil Gurijala, Ph.D.
Nortel Networks

The Systems Engineering (SE) is an age-old discipline successfully applied to several technologies including Telecommunications. SE functions, like requirements analysis, analytical and simulation modeling, resource bottleneck identification and analysis, lab verification, field monitoring and data analysis for capacity, performance, overload, reliability and traffic engineering, have been significant part of the Telecommunications networks. So far, the SE processes and techniques have been developed and evolved independently for different telecommunication technologies: Wireline and Wireless; Voice, Video and Data; TDM and IP. As the emerging telecommunication networks and services leaning towards Convergence, SE functions also needs to be converged. The complexity arises due to the fact that the characteristics and requirements of different services and applications are different and it’s a challenge to engineer a system or a network that meets all the requirements. This presentation discusses these challenges and how they are being addressed.
