Evaluation Criteria for Software Processes
Ranjit ‘Raj’ Singh
Lockheed Martin Aeronautics Company

There has been a significant amount of discussion and debate on the characteristics of good software development processes over the decades of software development. There are several characteristics common to value-add processes that distinguish them from processes that are perceived to be detrimental and cumbersome.

This presentation will discuss the criteria to use to evaluate processes, and specify requirements that process definitions need to follow in order to be value-add, complete, and manageable from the perspectives of both the end-user of the process and management.

Raj Singh is the software product manager for the F-22 Communications Software at Lockheed Martin Aeronautics in Ft Worth. He held a variety of positions developing embedded software and then managing software development at Motorola and 3Com prior to joining Lockheed Martin.

Raj has a B.S. in Software Engineering from The Ohio State University, an M.S. in Computer Science from Illinois Institute of Technology, and is currently in the process of getting an M.B.A. from Southern Methodist University.
