Ad Hoc Software Interfacing: Enterprise Application Integration when Middleware is Overkill
Arthur Reyes, UTA
8 :30-9:30

Abstract: Enterprise application integration (EAI) is cooperation of disparate systems and components to implement business rules in a distributed environment. "Systems and components" can be computer-aided design (CAD) or software engineering (CASE) tools, enterprise databases, COTS applications, or in-house software. Ad hoc software interfacing (AHSI) is a special kind of EAI. A tradeoff analysis classifies an EAI problem as an AHSI problem when middleware solutions are seen as heavy-handed. I.e., the planned EAI is not expected to become broad enough to justify the generality of a middleware solution. AHSI seeks to "wire" extant software applications as components in new, larger software applications. We call applications-as-components "appliponents". AHSI seeks to minimize recoding of appliponents to the greatest extent possible. We demonstrate solutions to AHSI problems using XML toolkits, domain-specific language toolkits, and Microsoft BizTalk Server.

Biography: Reyes received his B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from polytechnic University in 1987. Reyes worked for Northern Corporation from 1987 to 1990 on the B-2 stealth Bomber aircrew training services. Reyes received the PhD in Information and Computer Science from University of California at Irvine in 1999. Reyes has been employed as assistant Professor with CSE@UTA since Sept. 1999. http://ranger.uta.edu/~reyes/research/reyes_biography.html

Adaptive Virtual Environments - Applications and Opportunities
Karl Steiner, UNT CS
9 :45-10:45

Abstract: Virtual environments (or VEs) are a powerful option for visualizing and interacting with information, yet their widespread adoption has been limited at least in part by the expense and difficulty of producing complex and engaging VE applications. However, research is providing tools and techniques for simplifying the production and management of VE experiences. We will provide a review of recent advances in adaptive VEs, as well as discuss potential applications of this technology to business, education, and entertainment.

Biography: Karl Steiner is an assistant professor of computer science at the University of North Texas. He has both a research and professional background in Human-Computer Interaction, with particular interests in Intelligent Interfaces for Virtual Environments, Computer Supported Collaborative Work, and Interactive Narratives. Dr. Steiner received his Ph.D. in computer science from the University of Illinois (Chicago). In addition to supervising research-oriented efforts, Dr. Steiner has also consulted on many industry projects. He can be reached at steiner@cs.unt.edu.

Community Computing Framework for Enhancing Internet Services
Mohan Kumar, UTA
11:00-12:00

Abstract: The Internet is expected to be employed to facilitate such applications as telemedicine, education, and transportation, military all the time and everywhere. These applications demand automated, continual unobtrusive services and proactive real-time collaborations among devices and software agents in dynamic heterogeneous environments. The existing push-/pull-based Internet and network model which is intended essentially for static information services is inadequate to meet these requirements. In this talk we present a novel framework based on communities of agents to enhance the availability of Internet services to users and applications.

Biography: Mohan Kumar is an associate professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Texas at Arlington. His research areas include pervasive computing, mobile computing and the wirless Internet and parallel and distributed computing. He has a PhD in computer science from the Indian Institute of Science. He is a senior member of the IEEE. Contact him at the Dept. of Computer Science and Eng., PO Box 19015, Arlington, TX 76019; kumar@cse.uta.edu.

End-to-End Enterprise Application Integration with the Microsoft .NET Framework and BizTalk 2004
Brian Moore, Microsoft
William Oellermann, Avanade
1:30-3:00

Abstract: With the advent of Web Services, many businesses are looking into deploying Web Services as a component of their enterprise application integration (EAI) strategy. Given the ease of development and deployment of Web Services in the Microsoft .NET Framework, the Microsoft platform is being considered more and more by businesses at the foundation of their EAI strategy. However, savvy businesses are looking to the future to see how Web Services can be exploited as a part of the larger business process. This session will explore building solutions that automate business processes and integrate web services utilizing BizTalk Server 2004 and the Microsoft .NET Framework.

Wireless-to-Wired Network Controls and Security
Jeff Carrell, HP-ProCurve Networking Division
3:15-4:45

Abstract: With more and more mobile computing (from home to enterprise) users and companies deploying wireless LANs (WLAN), security and access controls are the most often abused, as the technology in place today is limited and easy to compromise. There are a number of WLAN security standards being developed (led by IEEE 802.11 working groups), but they don't solve all the issues in an overall secure WLAN architecture.

This session will cover security challenges of deploying a WLAN, client OS capabilities of security access & controls, the technologies used to meet data privacy requirements of the WLAN, and methodologies used to safeguard network integrity. In addition, there will be a real-time demonstration of an end-to-end secure WLAN.

Biography: Jeff has been in the internetworking business for over 17 years and the computer industry for 24 years. Having designed thousands of networks all over the world, Jeff has worked for both manufacturers and end-users, been an independent network consultant, enjoys "playing" with the latest "cool" technology, and maintains his own computer museum spanning 25 years of working microcomputers.

Jeff is active in the IEEE Fort Worth Section having served in various Executive Committee postions.
